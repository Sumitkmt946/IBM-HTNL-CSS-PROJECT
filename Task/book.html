The Great Gatsby

by F. Scott Fitzgerald

Rating: ★★★★☆

A captivating tale of wealth, love, and the American Dream. Fitzgerald's prose is poetic and poignant, weaving a story that resonates deeply about the complexities of life in the Jazz Age.

1984

by George Orwell

Rating: ★★★★★

A chilling dystopian novel that explores themes of totalitarianism, surveillance, and individuality. Orwell's portrayal of a society where truth is manipulated and freedom is suppressed remains incredibly relevant today.

To Kill a Mockingbird

by Harper Lee

Rating: ★★★★☆

An unforgettable story of racial injustice, innocence, and courage in the American South. The novel provides a powerful exploration of morality, compassion, and the complexities of human nature.

The Catcher in the Rye

by J.D. Salinger

Rating: ★★★☆☆

A rebellious journey through the mind of Holden Caulfield, a young man struggling with the world around him. Salinger's portrayal of alienation, disillusionment, and self-exploration is poignant, yet somewhat detached.
